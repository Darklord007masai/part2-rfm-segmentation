# Strategic Customer Segmentation & Capital Prioritization Report

## 1. Segment Design Justification
We have grouped our 2,400 customers into 5 mutually exclusive operational segments[cite: 2, 3]:

*   **Champions:** Customers with optimal recency, frequency, and monetary scores ($R \ge 4, F \ge 4, M \ge 4$). They represent our core brand advocates and drive the highest revenue share.
*   **Loyal / Core Buyers:** Reliable mid-tier customers who maintain steady, predictable interaction habits.
*   **At-Risk Customers:** Historically active buyers who have not completed an order recently ($R \le 2, F \ge 3$). They are at a critical decision point for brand abandonment.
*   **Frustrated / High-Complaint:** Customers flagged directly by non-RFM signals—specifically holding an order return rate over 40% or logging multiple support tickets within the past 90 days ($recent\_tickets \ge 2$)[cite: 2, 3].
*   **Dormant Customers:** Extreme low-engagement accounts exhibiting minimal transactional velocity ($F \le 1$) or long periods of complete non-activity.

---

## 2. Retention Actions by Segment Matrix

| Segment Name | Risk Priority | Core Operational Retention Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Champions** | Low | VIP early-access events; new collection product testers; zero direct discount dependency. |
| **Loyal / Core Buyers** | Medium | Periodic replenishment reminders; cross-category recommendations based on historical preferences. |
| **At-Risk Customers** | High | High-value, personalized win-back offers (e.g., flat ₹300 off their preferred product category). |
| **Frustrated / High-Complaint** | Urgent | Direct Customer Support outreach; priority resolution processing; refund acceleration. |
| **Dormant Customers** | Low | Low-cost email re-engagement tracks; automated seasonal coupon pushes. |

---

## 3. Limited Budget Prioritization Framework
Given a strictly limited retention budget, **At-Risk Customers** and **Frustrated / High-Complaint Customers** must be prioritized first.

*   **Why?** *Champions* do not require immediate discounts to maintain active purchase behavior, and *Dormant* profiles have a low conversion probability that risks wasting marketing capital. 
*   **The Strategy:** High-complaint users represent immediate churn risks due to operational issues. Resolving their issues can save their customer lifetime value (LTV). Similarly, *At-Risk* customers already understand our brand value; a targeted, time-sensitive incentive is highly effective at re-engaging them before they fully transition into dormancy.
